“Good [morning/afternoon/evening] everyone.

In this session, we’re going to focus on a critical aspect of AKS platform engineering—policy enforcement in a multi-tenant cluster.

As you know, in a shared cluster model, we need strong guardrails around things like security controls, resource governance, and workload isolation, while still enabling teams to deploy independently.

The key question we’re addressing today is:
where and how should these controls be enforced in the Kubernetes control plane?

We’ll evaluate three approaches:

ValidationAdmissionPolicy as a native admission control mechanism,
Azure Policy add-on for centralized governance and compliance, and
Kyverno for Kubernetes-native policy-as-code.

We’ll go beyond features and look at architecture trade-offs—including extensibility, operational overhead, integration with Azure, and impact on developer workflows.
